Education by Social Service


In India, the idea of involving students in the task of national service dates back to the times of Mahatma Gandhi, the father of the nation. The central theme which he tried to impress upon his student audience time and again, was that they should always keep before them, their social responsibility. In 1960, at the instance of the Government of India, Prof. K.G. Saiyidain studied national service by students implemented in several countries of the world and submitted his report under the title "National Service for the Youth" to the Government with a number of recommendations as to what could be done in India to develop a feasible scheme of social service by students. It was also recommended that social service camps should be open to students as well as non-students within the prescribed age group for better inter-relationship. Initiatives and Collaborations

NSS MEC has proposed a few initiatives with the goal of improving the well-being of the community in which we live. We also collaborate with various social clubs of MEC and work intensely for a happy and healthy society.

Learn More

Pass The Pulse

The NSS unit of MEC aims at building a strong and healthy society. Blood donation camps are frequently arranged. The students step forward to support the cause and provide blood when needed. We regularly meet the requests from various hospitals in and around the district.

SHAIR

“Gift hair, gift confidence” It is an initiative in which we donate our hair to cancer patients who lost their hair during treatment. A well-made wig of human hair can increase their confidence as well as their self-esteem and also take them on the road to recovery.

KTU Care

KTU Care is a flagship activity of APJAKTU NSS Cell. It's a palliative care initiative which helps volunteers in acquiring empathy skills in place of merely sympathizing about ill-fated human beings among us.
